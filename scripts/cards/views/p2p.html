At PayPal, I worked on the P2P team and owned the web experiences for PayPal’s main non-merchant products. In particular we had three primary responsibilities: the main P2P transfer app at PayPal.com (above photo), a service called PayPal.me, and PayPal Money Pools (below photo — “pools” of money that can be collected for vacation, group-sharing Christmas gifts, or for fundraisers). We supported tens of millions of unique users every month.

I was writing server and client side JS code daily. Our team placed an emphasis on testing, and communication. We often had tech talks/lunches where I shared about new technologies, or a deep dive about some existing internal tool. Every pull request required multiple reviewers and "enough" conversation, and we frequently tackled projects together. That level of collaboration is important to me on any team. As one of the largest JS teams at PayPal, we worked together with Web Infra teams to make internal tooling better (early adoption of core modules, etc).

I worked closely with designers and product managers to grow the service globally, and to add major features. In addition to product work, I interviewed and trained new hires.
