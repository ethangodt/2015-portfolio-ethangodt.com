_ABOUT

I'm a designer with three years experience designing responsive, hi-res websites for local businesses. I’m currently looking to transition to an entry level developer position doing design, layout, and front-end application development.

I designed and developed this site to showcase some of my previous work with LaunchBot Creative — a (very) small web development business I started with a developer-friend — and to use the portfolio itself to demonstrate my understanding of modern, front-end development practices. I’d love to discuss what went into it, and what problems needed to be solved.

My first foray into the broad category of digital media was through Photoshop. As a result, I am very, very comfortable using it. I am an Adobe Certified Expert of Photoshop, and my comfortability allows me to explore artistic ideas that would be unintuitive to explore directly through a browser. Alongside photoshop, I have many hours of experience using Illustrator for vector graphics, InDesign for print layout, and AfterEffects for small motion graphics projects or for prototyping web animations.

Over the past couple years I’ve grown very interested in a development position so that I can be more wholly involved with the end product. To accomplish this I have been self-learning from the plethora of educational sites on the web. I am hoping to work with a team who is hardworking and innovative, but also unpretentious. I’m looking for a group that can take me and my strong supplemental skills, and train me to work in a collaborative development environment using best practices for version control, build processing, and test driven development.

_DESIGN

PlantMidwest is a new St. Louis religious organization that meets quarterly in several cities throughout the midwest. Through LaunchBot Creative I was able to design their first website. The site needed to describe the organization, make it very easy to sign up for their quarterly events, and catalog media from each of those events for later viewing. We worked with the client for a few months altering page designs and functionality, and eventually landed on a final product that is one of the best sites that we delivered. The home page above and the image clips below are a sample of the muted and modern feel of the final design.

Though LaunchBot Creative was primarily a custom web development business, we frequently helped organizations finalize logos to pair with new websites. I worked with a staff content director at PlantMidwest to bring some of his logo ideas to fruition. The logo below is the collaborative end product.

CrossFit St. Charles is the prominent CrossFit gym in the St. Charles area, and as CrossFit gained popularity over the past couple years they very quickly outgrew their Typepad "drag and drop" site. They very much needed something developed that felt more professional. I was able to design a site for them which better enabled them to do custom outreach to their members, which better fit the color scheme and branding of their building’s signage, and which didn’t look like a Typepad site…

Ekklesia is a small church in St. Charles that needed a first website. They had been previously relying on a podcasting app to share messages and news, so we built them a responsive site that they transitioned to using in junction with social media for communication. The site was fully responsive, designed for high resolution displays (like all of our sites), and included Google maps integration for community groups and media management for weekly sermons.
